## 🎯 Strategic Overview

Our refined persona landscape targets **all job seekers in the DACH market**, with **specialized expertise in regulated industries (pharma, healthcare, medtech)** as a premium differentiation layer. This positions Apliqa as "the CV platform so precise it works for regulated industries" — making it attractive to every user.

| Persona    | Type      | Strategic Role                         | Revenue Model        | Market Segment |
| ---------- | --------- | -------------------------------------- | -------------------- | --- |
| **Marcus** | Human     | Core revenue, validates tailoring      | Premium subscription | All industries (with pharma option) |
| **Priya**  | Human     | Cultural differentiation, SEO traction | Mid-tier pricing     | International relocators (all industries) |
| **Emma**   | Human     | Retention & upsell, highest LTV        | Premium + add-ons    | Returning power users |
| **Jason**  | Human     | Recruiter, Power user, B2B             | B2B customer         | Specialist recruiters (all industries) |
| **Dr. Weber** | Human  | Premium layer, regulated industries    | Industry Packs       | Pharma/GxP/Medtech specialists |
| **Kaile**  | AI Agent  | Tech moat, agent-native workflows      | API credits          | AI-delegated workflows |

**Key Changes in v2.0:**
✅ **Broadened Marcus & Priya** – Now represent all DACH professionals, not just pharma/regulated industries

✅ **Aligned with Business Plan v2.0** – Reflects "sword not fence" positioning

## 👔 Persona 1: "The Experienced Professional"

**Name:** Marcus (archetype)
**Age:** 35-50
**Location:** DACH region
**Career stage:** 10-20+ years in **any industry** (software, manufacturing, pharma, finance, automotive, etc.)
**Job titles:** Senior Engineer, Manager, Specialist, Lead, Director
**Optional specialization:** Pharma/Healthcare/Medtech compliance background

### 🔍 Demographics & Situation

Marcus has built a **multi-domain career** across various industries:
- Started in **software development** or **engineering** (e.g., LIMS, MES, SAP, manufacturing systems)
- Moved into **system ownership** or **technical leadership**
- Transitioned into **management, QA, or specialized roles**
- Now leads a **team or department** or is a **senior individual contributor**

His LinkedIn shows **5-8 positions across 3-4 employers**. He has **2-4 old CVs** saved on his laptop, each tailored for a different type of role. None are current.

He's **selectively exploring** new opportunities:
- A recruiter reached out about a **senior role**
- He spotted an interesting **leadership position** on LinkedIn
- He's considering a **career pivot** or **lateral move**

The role he's targeting emphasizes a **different facet of his experience** than his last application did.

**Optional: Pharma/GxP Background**
If Marcus has pharma/healthcare/medtech experience, his CVs emphasize:
- Compliance experience (GxP, ISO 13485, 21 CFR Part 11)
- Audit history (FDA, EMA, internal audits)
- SOP and documentation expertise
- Validation projects

### 🎯 Jobs to Be Done

- **"Help me extract the RIGHT experience for THIS specific role"**
  *(e.g., "This job wants 21 CFR Part 11 expertise—highlight my blood bank work" OR "This job wants cloud architecture—highlight my AWS projects")*
- **"Don't make me rewrite my CV from scratch every time I target a different type of position"**
- **"Remember everything about my career—including projects I forgot—so I only have to do the hard work once"**
- **"Surface hidden relevant experience I have but don't think to mention"**
  *(e.g., "Your work with electronic batch records implies GAMP 5 knowledge" OR "Your DevOps experience is directly relevant for this SRE role")*

### ⚠️ Pain Points

1. **The curation problem**
   - He has **too much experience**, not too little.
   - The challenge is **selecting and prioritizing** the right details for each role.
2. **The multi-version nightmare**
   - Maintaining **3-4 CV variants manually** is unsustainable.
   - Copy-pasting between versions introduces **errors and inconsistencies**.
3. **The buried relevance problem**
   - His work at a **previous company 8 years ago** is highly relevant for this role, but he'd never think to highlight it.
4. **Time cost**
   - Each manually tailored CV takes **4-6 hours**.
   - At his seniority level, that time has **real opportunity cost**.

### 💰 Willingness to Pay

**HIGH** (€5–10 per tailored CV)
- Likely to generate **5–10 CVs per active job search**.
- Potential **lifetime value: €100+**.
- Would pay for **premium features** (e.g., cover letter generation, ATS scoring, mock interviews).

## 👩‍💼 Persona 2: "The International Relocator"

**Name:** Priya (archetype)
**Age:** 28-45
**Location:** Moving **to DACH** (from India, Brazil, US, Turkey, etc.)
**Career stage:** 5–15 years in **any industry** (software, pharma, engineering, finance, etc.)
**Job titles:** Software Engineer, Scientist, Engineer, Specialist, Associate, Manager
**Optional specialization:** Pharma, biotech, or medtech background

### 🔍 Demographics & Situation

Priya has **secured a job offer** or is **actively applying** to roles in **Germany, Austria, or Switzerland**. She has a **strong CV**—but it's formatted for her **home market**:
- **India:** 5-page CV with **personal details** (photo, father's name, marital status)
- **US:** 1-page resume with **no personal information**
- **Brazil:** Currículo Vitae with **references listed upfront**

She's Googling:
- *"How to write a German CV"*
- *"Should I include a photo on my German CV?"*
- *"How to present my Indian degree in Germany"*
- *"CV format for [my industry] in Germany"*

She doesn't know that **German CVs emphasize**:
✅ **Reverse-chronological format** (not functional or skills-based)
✅ **Professional photo** (optional but expected in regulated industries)
✅ **Credential mapping** (translating degrees, certifications)
✅ **Language proficiency** (German/English levels)
✅ **Work authorization** (visa status clarity)

**Optional: Pharma/Life Sciences Background**
If Priya has pharma/biotech/medtech experience, she additionally needs to understand:
- **Compliance language** (GxP, ISO 13485, 21 CFR Part 11)
- **Regulatory framework mapping** (ANVISA → EU GMP, FDA → EMA)
- **Audit experience** positioning

### 🎯 Jobs to Be Done

- **"Tell me what a German employer expects to see in my CV"**
- **"Reformat my experience into the right structure for this market"**
- **"Don't let me make cultural mistakes that get my CV rejected before anyone reads it"**
- **"Help me present my international experience as a strength—not a question mark"**
  *(e.g., "Your work with Brazilian ANVISA regulations is relevant for EU MDR" OR "Your US tech stack is directly applicable in Germany")*
- **"Translate my credentials so German HR understands them"**

### ⚠️ Pain Points

1. **Cultural blindness**
   - She doesn't know what she doesn't know.
   - Her CV might be **excellent by home-country standards** but **completely wrong for German employers**.
2. **Conflicting online advice**
   - Every blog says something different about **German CV conventions**.
3. **The photo question**
   - Should she include one? What kind? What about **anti-discrimination concerns**?
4. **Language barrier**
   - The job is in English, but should the CV be in **German**?
   - What about the **Lebenslauf format**?
5. **Credential translation**
   - How to present a **B.Tech from IIT or a Brazilian Bacharelado** in a way German HR understands?

### 💰 Willingness to Pay

**MODERATE-HIGH** (€5–8 per CV)
- The pain is **acute**, and she's relocating for a **high-paying job**.
- Likely **3–5 CVs per job search**.
- Would pay for **cultural adaptation guides** or **industry-specific templates**.

## 👩‍💻 Persona 3: "The Returning Power User"

**Name:** Emma (archetype)
**Age:** 35-50
**Location:** DACH region
**Career stage:** 10+ years in **any industry**
**Job titles:** Senior roles, specialists, leads
**Usage:** **Returning user** (has generated multiple CVs with Apliqa)

### 🔍 Demographics & Situation

Emma has used Apliqa **multiple times** to generate tailored CVs. Her **Master Profile is 80–100% complete**, and she **trusts the system**.

She's now applying to **2–3 senior roles simultaneously** and expects:
✅ **Maximum automation** (minimal manual input)
✅ **Consistency** (no errors or omissions)
✅ **Speed** (CVs generated in **minutes, not hours**)

She's not just a user—she's a **power advocate**:
- Might **refer colleagues**
- Could use Apliqa for her **entire team** (B2B potential)

### 🎯 Jobs to Be Done

- **"One-Click Tailoring"**
  - Paste a job description → get a **perfectly tailored CV in seconds**.
- **Version control**
  - Track which CV she sent to which employer.
- **Delta view**
  - See **what changed** from her last CV—and why.
- **Proactive updates**
  - *"You haven't added your new project—should we update your Master Profile?"*

### ⚠️ Pain Points

1. **The "I already did this" problem**
   - She doesn't want to **re-enter data** she's already provided.
2. **The "what changed?" problem**
   - She needs to **audit her own CV** for consistency.
3. **The "I forgot something" problem**
   - She might overlook a **recent certification or project** that's highly relevant.

### 💰 Willingness to Pay

**VERY HIGH** (highest LTV)
- Likely to:
  - Upgrade to a **premium subscription** (€20–30/mo)
  - Use **add-ons** (e.g., cover letter generation, ATS scoring, mock interviews)
  - Refer colleagues (**B2B team plan potential**)

## 🤖 Persona 4: "The Autonomous AI Agent"

**Name:** Kaile (archetype)
**Type:** **Non-human** (LLM-based agent)
**Owner:** Marcus, Priya, or Emma (human user)
**Use case:** **Fully automated CV tailoring**

### 🔍 Demographics & Situation

Kaile is a **Claude, GPT-4o, or custom LLM agent** that Marcus, Priya, or Emma has **delegated** to generate a tailored CV for a specific job posting.

The human user provides:
- A **job description** (URL or text)
- Access to their **Apliqa Master Profile**
- Optional: **Custom instructions** (e.g., *"Emphasize my 21 CFR Part 11 experience"*)

Kaile then:
1. **Parses the job description** for keywords and requirements
2. **Retrieves the user's Master Profile**
3. **Generates a tailored CV** (and optionally a cover letter)
4. **Returns the output** to the user (or directly submits it)

### 🎯 Jobs to Be Done (for the Agent)

- **"Generate a CV for this job posting—no manual work required."**
- **"Match my experience to the job requirements automatically."**
- **"Ensure the output is ATS-friendly and culturally appropriate."**
- **"Do this at scale—I'm applying to 10 jobs this week."**

### ⚙️ Technical Requirements

- **Structured input/output** (JSON-based, not PDF)
- **Deterministic behavior** (reproducible results, no hallucinations)
- **API rate limits & credit system** (metered usage)
- **Clear documentation** (for developers integrating with Apliqa)

### 💰 Monetization

Kaile **does not pay directly**—instead, the **human user (Marcus, Priya, or Emma) pays via API credits**:
- Marcus pays **€20/mo** for **20 API credits**
- Each tailored CV costs **1 credit**
- If he runs out, he **buys more credits**

## 👔 Persona 5: "The Specialist Headhunter"

**Name:** Jason (archetype)
**Age:** 35–55
**Location:** DACH region (Frankfurt, Munich, Zurich, Vienna)
**Career stage:** 8–20+ years in executive search / specialist recruitment
**Job titles:** Senior Recruiter, Partner, Managing Director at a boutique recruitment agency
**Specialisation:** Pharma, Life Sciences, MedTech, Healthcare IT, GxP-regulated industries (but increasingly across all industries)

---

### 🔍 Demographics & Situation

Jason runs a boutique recruitment firm (2–10 consultants) or is a senior partner at a mid-sized agency specialising in regulated industries. His clients are pharmaceutical companies, CROs, medtech manufacturers, and healthcare IT providers across the DACH region.

He operates on a contingency or retained search model, compensated only on successful placement. A typical placement fee is 20–30% of the candidate's annual salary — for a €120k Director role, that's **€24k–€36k per placement**.

### Candidate Pool

- 200–500 active profiles in his database
- 5–15 open mandates running simultaneously
- 3–5 new CVs arriving daily from candidates or sourced via LinkedIn/XING

### Daily Reality

- Receives a CV from a candidate (PDF/DOCX, often in the candidate's own format)
- Reviews for DACH compliance: reverse-chronological structure, professional photo, no unexplained gaps, Zeugnisse attached
- Reformats into his agency's **Kandidatenprofil** template — the branded, standardised document he submits to clients
- Highlights the experience that matches each client's specific JD
- For sensitive mandates, anonymises the profile (blind submission: strip name, photo, contact details)
- Submits the Kandidatenprofil (not the original CV) to the hiring company
- Tracks pipeline status across multiple mandates, clients, and candidates

> **Time cost:** Each Kandidatenprofil takes 30–60 minutes to create manually. At 3–5 profiles per day, that's **2.5–5 hours of daily formatting work** — his highest-cost, lowest-value activity.

### Tech Stack Today

| Tool | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Starhunter / Hunter | CRM/ATS (candidate database, project tracking) |
| Word templates | Kandidatenprofil formatting (manual copy-paste) |
| LinkedIn Recruiter | Sourcing |
| Excel | Pipeline tracking |
| regrooter (if tech-forward) | Basic AI-assisted profile creation |

### His Frustration

The tools he uses are either dumb template fillers (no industry intelligence) or enterprise ATS platforms (too heavy, too expensive, not tailored for specialist search). None of them understand that a candidate's "blood bank IT lead" experience is directly relevant for a "QA Manager, 21 CFR Part 11" mandate. **He's doing the intelligence work himself, every time.**

---

### 🎯 Jobs to Be Done

- "Turn this CV into a branded Kandidatenprofil for my client — in 2 minutes, not 45"
- "Highlight the regulatory experience that matters for THIS specific mandate" *(e.g., "This client needs GAMP 5 expertise — surface it from the candidate's MES project work")*
- "Anonymise this profile for a blind submission — strip PII but keep the substance"
- "I have 8 new CVs and 3 open mandates — show me which combinations are worth pursuing" *(V2 — Regulated Zone)*
- "Track my entire pipeline — who was submitted where, what's the status, where are the gaps"
- "Let my AI assistant handle the routine Kandidatenprofil generation while I focus on client relationships" *(Kaile delegation)*

---

### ⚠️ Pain Points

#### The Reformatting Tax
- 30–60 minutes per Kandidatenprofil, 3–5 per day = **2.5–5 hours of daily formatting work**
- His highest-cost, lowest-value activity
- Every hour spent formatting is an hour NOT spent on client relationships or candidate sourcing

#### The Intelligence Gap
- His CRM stores candidate data but doesn't understand it
- He knows that "blood bank" implies 21 CFR Part 11, that "MES implementation" implies GAMP 5 — but his tools don't
- He manually surfaces relevant experience for each mandate, relying on memory and intuition

#### The Batch Problem
- When a new mandate comes in, he needs to review his entire candidate pool against it
- Currently done manually — scrolling through profiles, relying on memory
- No systematic way to identify "which of my 300 candidates might fit this role?"

#### The Tracking Chaos
- Pipeline management across multiple mandates, clients, and candidates
- Excel spreadsheets, email threads, sticky notes
- No single source of truth for "who was submitted where, and what happened?"

#### The Compliance Burden
- GDPR consent management for candidate data
- EU AI Act awareness (he knows it's coming but doesn't know what it means for his tools)
- Zeugnisse verification, reference checks, documentation requirements

---

### 💰 Willingness to Pay

> **VERY HIGH** (highest per-unit value across all personas)

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Single placement fee | €24,000 – €36,000 |
| Time saved per Kandidatenprofil | 30–45 minutes |
| Daily time savings | 2–3 hours |
| Weekly capacity increase | 10–15 hours of client-facing work |

#### Pricing Tolerance

- **B2B team plan:** €29.99/mo base + per-Kandidatenprofil credits (€2–5 per generated profile)
- At 100 profiles/month: €230–530/month — trivial against his revenue
- **Enterprise tier potential:** Agencies with 10+ consultants could pay €500–1,000/month

#### Lifetime Value Projection

A single successful placement (€30k fee) dwarfs any SaaS subscription. If Apliqa helps him close **ONE additional placement per year**, ROI is **50–100x**.

## 🧪 Persona 6: "The Pharma/GxP Specialist" (Premium Layer)

**Name:** Dr. Weber (archetype)
**Age:** 40–60
**Location:** DACH region (Germany, Switzerland, Austria)
**Career stage:** 15–25+ years in **pharma, medtech, biotech, or healthcare IT**
**Job titles:** Head of QA, Validation Manager, Regulatory Affairs Director, GMP Lead, Compliance Officer, IT Compliance Specialist
**Specialisation:** GxP (GMP, GLP, GCP), 21 CFR Part 11, EU GMP Annex 11, ISO 13485, MDR/IVDR, CSV/CSA frameworks

### 🔍 Demographics & Situation

Dr. Weber is a **highly specialized professional** with deep expertise in regulated industries. Unlike Marcus (who may have pharma experience as one facet of a broader career), Dr. Weber's **entire career** is built on GxP compliance, validation, and regulatory affairs.

He's:
- **Selective about opportunities** — only considers roles that value his specialized knowledge
- **High-earning** — his expertise commands premium salaries (€120k–€200k+)
- **Demanding about precision** — he knows exactly what a pharma CV should look like and won't tolerate mediocrity
- **Willing to pay for quality** — he understands that a poorly formatted CV could cost him a €150k opportunity

### 🎯 Jobs to Be Done

- **"Ensure my CV reflects my GxP expertise with precision and credibility"**
  - Highlight validation projects, audit experience, compliance frameworks
  - Position regulatory knowledge as a strategic asset
- **"Help me tailor my CV for different pharma roles"**
  - QA Manager vs. Regulatory Affairs vs. Validation Engineer — each requires different emphasis
- **"Provide industry-specific templates and guidance"**
  - What does a pharma CV look like? What are the red flags? What impresses pharma recruiters?
- **"Connect me with industry-specific opportunities"**
  - Job recommendations filtered for GxP/regulated industries

### ⚠️ Pain Points

1. **Generic tools don't understand pharma**
   - Standard CV builders treat "validation project" as just another job duty
   - They don't understand GAMP 5, 21 CFR Part 11, or why these matter
2. **Regulatory language is critical**
   - A CV that says "managed system implementation" is weaker than "led GAMP 5 validation for SAP ERP"
   - Generic tools can't make this distinction
3. **Pharma recruiters are specialized**
   - They know what to look for; a poorly formatted pharma CV signals incompetence
4. **Limited industry-specific resources**
   - Most CV advice is generic; pharma-specific guidance is rare

### 💰 Willingness to Pay

**VERY HIGH** (Premium tier)
- Would pay **€19.99–29.99/mo** for a **Pharma Career Pack** (industry-specific templates, guidance, job recommendations)
- Would pay **€5–10 per specialized CV generation** (with pharma-specific tailoring)
- Potential **lifetime value: €500+** (high-value user, long career)

### 🎁 Premium Features for Dr. Weber

- **Industry-Specific Templates:** "Pharma QA Manager CV", "Regulatory Affairs Director CV", "Validation Engineer CV"
- **Regulatory Framework Guidance:** Explanations of GAMP 5, 21 CFR Part 11, EU GMP Annex 11, etc.
- **Pharma Job Recommendations:** Curated job listings filtered for GxP/regulated industries
- **Pharma Recruiter Network:** Access to specialized recruiters in the pharma space
- **Mock Interviews for Pharma Roles:** AI-generated questions specific to pharma/GxP interviews
- **Compliance Checklist:** "Is your pharma CV compliant?" — automated review against pharma standards

## 🔑 Key Takeaways for Product & Marketing

1. **Master Profile is the core asset**
   - Emma's high LTV proves its value.
   - Invest in **proactive profile updates** (e.g., "You haven't added your new project").

2. **Broad market positioning with specialized depth**
   - Apliqa serves **all DACH professionals**.
   - Pharma/GxP expertise is a **proof of precision**, not a market restriction.
   - Marketing message: "So precise it works for regulated industries."

3. **Agent-native workflows (Kaile) are a competitive moat**
   - Start building **API support early**.
   - Position Apliqa as **"the first CV builder built for AI agents"**.

4. **Recruiter workflows (Jason) are high-value B2B**
   - Kandidatenprofil generation is the **killer feature** for Jason.
   - Time savings (30–45 min per profile) translate to **real revenue impact**.

5. **Cultural adaptation (Priya) is a strong differentiator**
   - Tied to **DACH-specific norms** (not just generic international advice).
   - Offer **cultural adaptation guides** as a premium feature.

6. **Retention > acquisition**
   - Emma's journey should be the **North Star for UX**.
   - Focus on **reducing friction for returning users** (e.g., one-click tailoring, version history).

7. **Premium layer (Dr. Weber) validates the business model**
   - Industry Packs (Phase 2) will unlock **high-value, high-margin revenue**.
   - Pharma expertise is the **first industry pack**; others (MedTech, Finance, etc.) follow.
